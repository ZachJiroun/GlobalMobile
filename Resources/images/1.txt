Egypt is a transcontinental country spanning the northeast corner of Africa and southwest corner of Asia. It is connected with a land bridge formed by the Sinai Peninsula. Egypt is a very dry nation due to its desert like terrain and climate. The Nile River is one of the largest rivers in the world, running from the north to south of the nation. The Nile River is used as an entry way to the nation, as well as a source of travel throughout the nation. A lot of trade and business occurs alongs the ports on the Nile. 

Egypt has a rich history, containing with the Great Pyramid of Giza, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Over time, this nation has been impacted by globalization. Egypt contains significant oil reserves. Due to its location next to Saudi Arabia, the worlds largest oil exporter, Egypt has the ability to compete in the oil market. When Egypt underwent its latest period of political crisis, it caused international investors to pull their money out of the country. The hope is that once the Egyptian government regains stability, the economy will stabilize and investments will flood back into the country.
