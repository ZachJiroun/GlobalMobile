El Salvador is the smallest and the most densely populated country in Central America. El Salvador borders the Pacific Ocean on the south, and the countries of Guatemala to the west and Honduras to the north and east. Its easternmost region lies on the coast of the Gulf of Fonseca, opposite Nicaragua. As of 2009, El Salvador had a population of composed predominantly of Mestizos.

El Salvador plays a vital role in the world economy. The first step they took was to adopt the US Dollar as their currency in 2001. In 2010 El Salvador ranked twelfth among Latin American countries in terms of the Human Development Index and fourth in Central America (behind Panama, Costa Rica, and Belize), due in part to ongoing rapid industrialization. On top of their exports and additions to the world economy they have other contributions. The El Salvadorian people have made their cuisine popular in the world. They have also contributed negatively to the global world by introducing MS-13, the worlds largest gang.
